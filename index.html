Tasty Spicy Rice Pilaf

The word pilaf orginated from Persia, where it was first recoreded in history.
Pilaf follows the migration patterns of ancient Persians through the Middle East, Asia and Europe.
From there, colonization brought pilaf to the Americans.

We hear pilaf and we think of rice, but there are styles, especially from the Middle East and Mediterranean that use bulgur, lentils, orzo or vermicelli.

Ingredients

Single Reciple Ingredients
2 tablespoons butter/oil
1 onion, diced
3/4 red pepper, diced
1 medium green pepper
6 mushrooms, chopped
3 cloves minced
2 cups uncooked instant rice
2 cups stock
2 teaspoons red pepper flakes
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper or other substitute

Steps/Methods

  1. Soak rice in boiled water for 5 minutes only.
  2. Wash out rice thoroughly and strain
  3. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat; cook an stir onion, half of the red pepper, mushrooms, and garlic until tender, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in rice.
  5. Add chicken stock; cover and boil until liquid is absorbed, about 10 minutes.
  6. Season with red pepper flakes, slt, and black pepper.
  7. Serve hot.
